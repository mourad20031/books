About the Book

The Subtle Art of Not Giving a F*ck by Mark Manson is a counterintuitive approach to living a good life. In this generation-defining self-help guide, Manson argues that we're living in an interesting time where we're more connected yet more anxious than ever. If you enjoyed this, check out our other self-improvement books.

Manson makes the argument that improving our lives hinges not on our ability to turn lemons into lemonade, but on learning to stomach lemons better. Human beings are flawed and limited, and the key to living a better life is not about giving a f*ck about everything, but rather about giving a f*ck only about what is true and immediate and important.

This #1 New York Times Bestseller offers a refreshing slap for a generation to help them lead contented, grounded lives. It cuts through the crap to show us how to be happier, more fulfilled people by being more selective about the f*cks we give.

What You'll Learn

How to stop trying to be positive all the time and become better at handling negative experiences

Why you're not special and how that realization can set you free
How to embrace uncertainty and the limitations of your knowledge
The value of suffering and how to find meaning in your struggles
How to take responsibility for everything that happens in your life
